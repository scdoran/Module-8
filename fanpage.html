Biography

The Mars Volta was an American progressive rock band from El Paso, Texas. Following the break-up of At the Drive-In, Omar Rodriguez-Lopez and Cedric Bixler-Zavala formed The Mars Volta in 2001. According to Rolling Stone, The Mars Volta was named the "Best Prog-Rock Band" in 2008. In 2009 the band won a Grammy Award in the Best Hard Rock Performance category for their song "Wax Simulacra".

The band released a total of 6 albums throughout its existence. The band eventually went on hiatus in 2012 and eventually broke up in 2014.

Final Members (as of 2012)

  • Cedric Bixler-Zavala: Vocals, lyrics
  • Juan Alderete: Bass
